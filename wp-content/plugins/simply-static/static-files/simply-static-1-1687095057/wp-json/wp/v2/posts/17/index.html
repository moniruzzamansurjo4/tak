Australian Usman Khawaja\u2019s mammoth innings came to an end when he was bowled for 141 by Ollie Robinson before lunch on day three of the first Ashes Test on Sunday, as the tourists just failed to surpass England\u2019s first-innings total of 393.<\/p>\n

Australia, who resumed on 311 for five on a warm, overcast morning at Edgbaston, lost both settled batters Khawaja and Alex Carey before three more wickets fell in quick succession as the visitors ended on 386 \u2013 seven runs short of England’s first innings score.<\/p>\n

Khawaja played with great composure for most of his knock, showcasing his experience as he picked off the singles and kept the ship steady.<\/p>\n

However, as England captain Ben Stokes piled on the pressure with an aggressive field placement comprised of six catchers, Khawaja took the bait, charging down the wicket before Robinson\u2019s inch-perfect yorker uprooted his off-stump.<\/p>\n

Earlier, James Anderson was denied Carey\u2019s wicket in the day\u2019s first over due to a Jonny Bairstow drop, but an angling delivery from the veteran bowler snared the Australian for 66 two overs later, ending his 118-run stand with Khawaja.<\/p>\n
